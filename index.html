About Me

I currently work as a Data Analytics / Modeling and Simulation Engineer at Leidos Dynetics. My role centers on the integration of physical models, simulation tools, and data analysis to support aerospace systems development.

I earned an M.S. in Aeronautics & Astronautics from Purdue University, where I studied critical joining technologies for hypersonic thermal protection systems.

Engineering & Research

My interests lie at the intersection of fundamental science and applied engineering—particularly in the domains of high-temperature materials, digital engineering, and systems-level modeling for extreme environments.

Other Interests

I’m inspired by the past and future of spaceflight, from Saturn V to modern reusable systems. Outside the lab, I pursue photography and music—fields that reflect the same balance of structure and improvisation I value in engineering.
